## The Healthcare Security Challenge

Healthcare organizations face unprecedented cybersecurity threats. Your patients trust you with their most sensitive information — their health records. That trust makes you a target.

---

## The Numbers Don't Lie

### Healthcare = #1 Target for Cyber Attacks

- **93%** of healthcare organizations experienced a data breach in the past 5 years
- **$10.1 million** — average cost of a healthcare data breach
- **70%** of breaches could be prevented with basic security measures
- **1 in 3** healthcare consumers affected by healthcare breaches

---

## Why Healthcare is Targeted

### 1. High-Value Data
Patient health records sell for up to **$100** on the dark web — 10x more than financial data.

### 2. Complex Systems
EHR systems, medical devices, cloud storage — many moving parts create vulnerabilities.

### 3. Regulatory Pressure
HIPAA fines can range from **$100 to $50,000** per violation.

### 4. Limited Resources
Small clinics and practices often lack dedicated IT and security teams.

---

## The Real Cost of a Breach

### Financial Impact
- Average breach cost: **$10.1 million**
- HIPAA violation fines: **$100 - $50,000+ per violation**
- Incident response and remediation: **$50,000 - $500,000**

### Operational Impact
- System downtime: days to weeks
- Revenue loss during recovery
- Staff time diverted to crisis management

### Reputational Impact
- Patient trust erosion
- Negative publicity
- Potential patient churn

---

## The Good News: Most Breaches Are Preventable

### Basic Security Measures Work

- ✅ Multi-factor authentication
- ✅ Regular security training
- ✅ Encrypted data storage
- ✅ Patch management
- ✅ Incident response planning
- ✅ Security risk assessments

These simple controls stop the majority of attacks.
