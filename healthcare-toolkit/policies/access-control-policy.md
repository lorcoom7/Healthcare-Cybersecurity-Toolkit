# Access Control Policy

## Healthcare Organization: _____________________________

---

## 1. Purpose

This Access Control Policy establishes requirements for controlling access to information systems and Protected Health Information (PHI) in compliance with HIPAA Security Rule §164.312(a).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and workforce members
- All systems that store, process, or transmit PHI
- All access points to organizational data

---

## 3. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Security Officer** | Approve access requests, conduct access reviews |
| **IT Administrator** | Implement access controls, manage user accounts |
| **Department Manager** | Validate access needs for team members |
| **Employees** | Use access only as needed, protect credentials |

---

## 4. Access Control Principles

### 4.1 Minimum Necessary
Access shall be limited to the minimum necessary to perform job functions.

### 4.2 Role-Based Access
Access is assigned based on job function and role.

### 4.3 Separation of Duties
Critical functions are divided among different individuals.

## 6. Access Levels

| Level | Description | Systems Access |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Level 1 - Administrative** | Full system administration | All systems, user management |
| **Level 2 - Clinical** | Patient care providers | EHR, clinical systems |
| **Level 3 - Administrative** | Business operations | Practice management, billing |
| **Level 4 - Read-Only** | Limited view access | Basic reporting |
| **Level 5 - External** | Business associates | Limited to BA systems |
